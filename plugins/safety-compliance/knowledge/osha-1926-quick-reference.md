## Subpart K — Electrical (1926.400-449)

| Section | Title | Key Requirements |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| 1926.402 | Applicability | Covers electrical safety during construction |
| 1926.404 | Wiring Design and Protection | GFCI protection on all 120V, 15/20A outlets on construction sites; assured equipment grounding |
| 1926.405 | Wiring Methods, Components, Equipment | Proper wiring methods; no spliced extension cords; strain relief |
| 1926.416 | Safety-Related Work Practices | Only qualified persons work on electric circuits; de-energize before work; lockout/tagout |
| 1926.417 | Lockout and Tagging of Circuits | Circuits de-energized and locked/tagged before work; verification of de-energization |
| 1926.431 | Maintenance of Equipment | Portable electric equipment maintained in safe condition |
| 1926.432 | Environmental Deterioration of Equipment | Equipment rated for environment (wet, corrosive, etc.) |
| 1926.449 | Definitions | Qualified person, competent person definitions |

### Key Requirements
- **GFCI protection** is required on ALL 120V, single-phase, 15/20-amp receptacle outlets on construction sites. No exceptions.
- Alternative: Assured Equipment Grounding Conductor Program (AEGCP) — but GFCI is preferred and simpler.
- Extension cords: no splices, proper grounding, 3-wire, rated for hard/extra-hard usage.
- Temporary wiring must be removed immediately upon completion of construction.

### Common Violations
- No GFCI protection on construction-site outlets
- Damaged extension cords (missing ground prong, cut insulation)
- Energized work without lockout/tagout
- Improper use of temporary wiring

## Subpart L — Scaffolds (1926.450-454)

| Section | Title | Key Requirements |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| 1926.450 | Scope, Application, Definitions | Applies to all scaffolds used in construction; definitions |
| 1926.451 | General Requirements | Capacity (4x intended load); platform width min 18"; guardrails at 10ft+; inspections by competent person |
| 1926.452 | Additional Requirements for Specific Scaffolds | Requirements by type (frame, mobile, suspended, etc.) |
| 1926.453 | Aerial Lifts | Boom/scissor lifts; body harness + lanyard to boom; no belt on boom |
| 1926.454 | Training | Trained by qualified person; recognize hazards; operate safely |

### Key Requirements
- **Guardrails**: Required on all open sides/ends of platforms > 10 ft above lower level
- **Top rail**: 38-45 inches; mid rail at midpoint; toeboard at platform
- **Capacity**: 4:1 safety factor minimum
- **Access**: Ladders, stairways, ramps — not cross braces
- **Planking**: Scaffold-grade lumber or manufactured planks; max gap 1 inch at platform edge
- **Competent person**: Must inspect before each work shift and after any event that could affect structural integrity
- **Aerial lifts**: Harness attached to boom or basket — NEVER to adjacent structure; stand on platform floor only

### Common Violations
- Missing guardrails on scaffold platforms
- No competent person inspections
- Cross-braces used as access (climbing on scaffold X-braces)
- Scaffold not fully planked
- Aerial lift: no harness, or harness attached to wrong anchor point

## Subpart M — Fall Protection (1926.500-503)

**The #1 cause of death and #1 most-cited standard in construction.**

| Section | Title | Key Requirements |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| 1926.500 | Scope, Application, Definitions | Applies to all construction workplaces; definitions of fall protection terms |
| 1926.501 | Duty to Have Fall Protection | 6-foot trigger height; specific requirements by activity |
| 1926.502 | Fall Protection Systems Criteria | Technical requirements for guardrails, nets, PFAS |
| 1926.503 | Training Requirements | Training by competent person; retraining when deficiencies; certification |

### 1926.501 — Trigger Heights by Activity

| Activity | Fall Protection Required At | Systems Allowed |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------|
| General construction (unprotected sides/edges) | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS |
| Leading edge work | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS, or fall protection plan |
| Hoist areas | 6 feet | Guardrail or PFAS |
| Holes (walking/working surfaces) | Any size if > 6 ft fall | Cover or guardrail |
| Formwork and reinforcing steel | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS |
| Ramps, runways, walkways | 6 feet | Guardrail |
| Excavations | 6 feet (not protected by sloping/benching) | Guardrail, fence, barricade |
| Dangerous equipment below | Any height | Guardrail or equipment guard |
| Overhand bricklaying | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS |
| Roofing - low-slope (< 4:12) | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS, warning line + monitor |
| Roofing - steep slope (> 4:12) | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS |
| Precast concrete erection | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS |
| Residential construction (certain conditions) | 6 feet | Guardrail, safety net, PFAS, or fall protection plan |
| Wall openings | 6 feet | Guardrail |
| Steel erection | 15 feet (per Subpart R) | PFAS (connectors: 15 ft / 2 bolts) |

### 1926.502 — System Requirements

**Guardrail Systems:**
- Top rail: 42 inches (+/- 3 in) above walking surface
- Mid rail: Midpoint between top rail and walking surface
- Withstand 200 lbs force at top rail (outward or downward)
- Toeboard: 3.5 inches minimum height

**Personal Fall Arrest Systems (PFAS):**
- Maximum arresting force: 1,800 lbs (with body harness)
- Maximum deceleration distance: 3.5 feet
- Maximum free fall distance: 6 feet
- Must bring employee to complete stop
- Anchor point: 5,000 lbs per person (or designed by qualified person for 2:1 safety factor)
- Full body harness required (no body belts for fall arrest)

**Safety Net Systems:**
- Maximum fall distance into net: 30 feet
- Installed as close as practicable below work surface
- Extend minimum 8-13 feet beyond edge (varies by fall distance)
- Drop test required: 400 lb, 28-32 inch diameter sand bag

### Common Violations
- No fall protection at 6+ feet (most cited construction violation)
- Inadequate or missing guardrails
- PFAS not properly inspected
- Anchor points inadequate (tied to conduit, vent pipes, etc.)
- No fall protection training / no documented training
- Holes not covered or guarded
- Warning line systems used without safety monitor (low-slope roofing)

| 1926.1432 | Multiple-Crane/Derrick Lifts | Pre-lift meeting, load distribution analysis, qualified person oversight |
| 1926.1434 | Equipment Modifications | No modifications without manufacturer approval |

### Key Requirements
- **Operator certification** required (NCCCO or equivalent) since February 2019
- **Power line clearance**: 20 feet minimum from lines up to 350 kV
- **Load chart** must be in cab and followed for every lift
- **Critical lift plan** for lifts > 75% of rated capacity or complex lifts
- **Signal person**: Qualified by third party or employer; hand signals or radio
- **Rigger**: Qualified by training/experience for rigging loads

### Common Violations
- Operator not certified
- Power line clearance violations (leading cause of crane electrocutions)
- No pre-shift inspection
- Exceeding rated capacity
- No signal person when operator cannot see load
- Swing radius not barricaded

## Subpart P — Excavations (1926.650-652)

**Second most-fatal construction hazard after falls.**

| Section | Title | Key Requirements |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| 1926.650 | Scope, Application, Definitions | Applies to all open excavations including trenches |
| 1926.651 | Specific Excavation Requirements | Utility locates, surface encumbrances, access/egress, water accumulation, stability of adjacent structures, fall protection, inspections |
| 1926.652 | Requirements for Protective Systems | Cave-in protection required > 5 feet depth (unless stable rock) |

### 1926.652 — Protection Methods

| Method | Description | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Sloping | Cut walls back to stable angle | Open areas with sufficient room |
| Benching | Step-cut walls in horizontal shelves | When full slope not practical |
| Shoring | Hydraulic, timber, or mechanical shores | Tight sites, urban, adjacent structures |
| Shielding (Trench Box) | Pre-manufactured protective structure | Standard trenching; most common method |

### Soil Classification and Maximum Allowable Slopes

| Soil Type | Description | Maximum Slope (H:V) |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Stable Rock | Natural solid mineral matter | Vertical (90 degrees) |
| Type A | Clay, silty clay, sandy clay (unconfined compressive strength >= 1.5 tsf) | 3/4:1 (53 degrees) |
| Type B | Silt, sandy loam, medium clay; previously disturbed Type A | 1:1 (45 degrees) |
| Type C | Gravel, sand, loamy sand, submerged soil, soil from which water is freely seeping | 1-1/2:1 (34 degrees) |

### Key Requirements
- **Competent person** must classify soil and inspect excavations daily and after every rain/vibration/event
- **Access/egress**: Ladder, ramp, or stairway within 25 feet of lateral travel for trenches 4+ feet deep
- **Utilities**: Contact 811 (One-Call) before digging; hand-dig within tolerance zone (18-24 inches per state)
- **Spoil**: Minimum 2 feet from edge of excavation
- **Water**: Remove accumulation before entry; control run-off

### Common Violations
- No cave-in protection in excavations > 5 feet deep
- No competent person present/assigned
- No access/egress (ladder) within 25 feet
- Spoil pile too close to edge
- No utility locates before digging

## Subpart X — Stairways and Ladders (1926.1050-1060)

| Section | Title | Key Requirements |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| 1926.1050 | Scope, Application, Definitions | Stairway or ladder required at all points of access where break in elevation > 19 inches |
| 1926.1051 | General Requirements | Stairway or ladder at every access point; no use of spiral stairways not designed for construction |
| 1926.1052 | Stairways | Stair rails/handrails; 30-50 degree angle; uniform riser height; landings |
| 1926.1053 | Ladders | 4:1 ratio (extension); 3 feet above landing; rungs 10-14 inch spacing; not loaded beyond capacity |
| 1926.1060 | Training | Train to recognize hazards; proper use of stairways and ladders |

### Key Requirements
- **Ladder setup**: 4:1 ratio (1 foot out for every 4 feet up)
- **Extension ladders**: Must extend 3 feet above landing surface
- **Step ladders**: Do not stand on top two steps; fully open and locked
- **Damaged ladders**: Must be removed from service immediately; tagged or destroyed
- **Stairways**: Handrails on open sides; stair rails 36 inches high (construction)

## Quick Reference — Construction Focus Four Hazards

OSHA's "Focus Four" hazards cause approximately 60% of all construction fatalities:

| Hazard | % of Fatal Four | Primary Standard | Key Prevention |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Falls | ~33% | 1926.501 (Subpart M) | Guardrails, PFAS, covers, training |
| Struck-By | ~11% | Various (Subparts N, O, Q) | Hard hats, high-viz, barricades, spotters |
| Caught-In/Between | ~6% | 1926.652 (Subpart P) | Trench protection, machine guarding, lockout |
| Electrocution | ~8% | 1926.404 (Subpart K) | GFCI, lockout/tagout, power line clearance |

## Competent Person Requirements Summary

Many OSHA standards require a "competent person" — defined as one who:
1. Is capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards
2. Is authorized to take prompt corrective measures to eliminate them

## OSHA Inspection Priorities

OSHA prioritizes inspections in this order:

1. **Imminent Danger** — Conditions that could cause death or serious harm immediately
2. **Fatalities/Catastrophes** — Within 8 hours of a death or 3+ hospitalizations
3. **Complaints/Referrals** — Worker complaints, referrals from other agencies
4. **Programmed Inspections** — Targeted industries/sites based on injury data
5. **Follow-up** — Verify correction of previous citations

### Employer Rights During Inspection
- Request warrant (but delays inspection; usually not recommended)
- Accompany inspector during walk-around
- Receive copies of all citations
- Contest citations within 15 working days
- Request informal conference with Area Director

### Employee Rights
- Request OSHA inspection anonymously
- Participate in walk-around inspection
- Access to exposure/medical records
- Protection from retaliation (Section 11(c))
