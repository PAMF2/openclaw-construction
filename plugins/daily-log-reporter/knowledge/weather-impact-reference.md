# Weather Impact Reference for Construction

> Thresholds, impacts, and required actions for weather conditions affecting construction operations.
> Based on OSHA regulations (29 CFR 1926), ACI standards, SMACNA guidelines, and industry best practice.

---

## Rain and Precipitation

### Impact Thresholds

| Condition | Threshold | Impact Level | Activities Affected |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Light rain | < 0.10 in/hr | LOW | Exterior painting, waterproofing, roofing |
| Moderate rain | 0.10 - 0.30 in/hr | MODERATE | Most exterior work, earthwork, concrete |
| Heavy rain | 0.30 - 0.50 in/hr | HIGH | All exterior work, crane operations, steel erection |
| Severe rain | > 0.50 in/hr | CRITICAL | All site work — suspend operations, secure site |
| Sustained rain | > 0.50 in cumulative/day | MODERATE-HIGH | Earthwork, foundations, grading |

### Activity-Specific Rain Restrictions

| Activity | Max Allowable Rain | Specification Basis | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Concrete placement | No standing water, no active rain | ACI 301, ACI 308 | Rain within 24 hrs of pour requires protection plan |
| Concrete finishing | Zero precipitation during finishing | ACI 302.1R | Surface must be free of bleed water before finishing |
| Structural steel erection | No rain that impairs visibility or grip | OSHA 1926.752 | Slippery conditions = stop work on iron |
| Roofing - membrane | Dry substrate required | Manufacturer specs | Most manufacturers void warranty if installed wet |
| Roofing - hot asphalt | No precipitation | NRCA guidelines | Water + hot asphalt = steam explosion risk |
| Exterior painting | No rain forecast 24 hrs after application | MPI standards | Surface must be dry; RH typically < 85% |
| Waterproofing | Dry substrate, no rain 24 hrs after | Manufacturer specs | Adhesion failure if substrate is wet |
| Masonry | No rain during mortar curing (24 hrs min) | ACI 530/TMS 402 | Rain washes uncured mortar from joints |
| Earthwork/grading | Light rain max | Project geotech | Wet soil cannot be compacted to spec |
| Compaction | No active rain | ASTM D698/D1557 | Moisture content must be within +/-2% of optimum |
| Asphalt paving | No rain, dry surface | AASHTO/DOT specs | Surface temp typically > 40-50F, dry pavement |
| Fireproofing (SFRM) | Interior only — protect from weather | UL listings | Spray fireproofing washes off if exposed to rain |
| Welding | No rain on weld area | AWS D1.1 | Moisture contamination weakens welds |
| Crane operations | Reduce capacity in rain | Manufacturer + OSHA | Reduced visibility, slippery loads; lightning = shut down |

### Stormwater / Erosion Control Requirements

| Rainfall Event | Required Action | Regulatory Basis |
|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| Any forecast rain | Inspect and maintain BMPs before event | NPDES/SWPPP (40 CFR 122) |
| > 0.25 in event | Inspect BMPs within 24 hrs after event | EPA CGP |
| > 0.50 in event | Document conditions, repair BMPs immediately | State NPDES permit |
| Sustained multi-day | Daily BMP inspections, reinforce controls | SWPPP requirements |

## Temperature — Cold Weather

### Impact Thresholds

| Condition | Threshold | Impact Level | Primary Concern |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Cool | 40-50F (4-10C) | LOW | Concrete curing slows, some coatings affected |
| Cold | 32-40F (0-4C) | MODERATE | Concrete protection required, many exterior activities limited |
| Freezing | 25-32F (-4 to 0C) | HIGH | Most wet trades affected, ice hazard |
| Severe cold | < 25F (< -4C) | CRITICAL | Concrete hot water/heating required, limited exterior work |
| Extreme cold | < 0F (< -18C) | CRITICAL | Minimal outdoor work, worker safety priority |

### Activity-Specific Cold Weather Restrictions

| Activity | Minimum Temperature | Standard | Required Protection |
|----------|-------------------|----------|-------------------|
| Concrete placement | 40F (4C) minimum and rising | ACI 306R | Cold weather plan required below 40F |
| Concrete curing | Maintain 50F (10C) for 48-72 hrs after pour | ACI 306R | Insulated blankets, heated enclosures, ground heaters |
| Concrete - high-early | 50F (10C) for 24 hrs minimum | ACI 306R | Use Type III cement or accelerators |
| Masonry | 40F (4C) and rising | ACI 530/TMS 602 | Heat mortar materials, protect wall for 24 hrs |
| Mortar mixing | Do not use frozen materials | TMS 602 | Heat sand, water; do not use antifreeze |
| Exterior painting | 50F (10C) minimum — check product TDS | MPI/Manufacturer | Surface and ambient must be above dew point |
| Waterproofing | Per manufacturer, typically 40-50F (4-10C) | Manufacturer | Most membranes require warm application |
| Asphalt paving | 40-50F (4-10C) surface temp minimum | State DOT specs | Compaction window shortens dramatically in cold |
| Roofing - membrane | 40F (4C) minimum for most products | Manufacturer | Adhesives fail below threshold; torch-applied may work lower |
| Roofing - SPF | 50F (10C) minimum | Manufacturer | Foam expansion affected by temperature |
| Steel erection | No minimum per OSHA, but practical limit ~0F | Industry practice | Worker safety, metal becomes brittle at extreme cold |
| Welding | Preheat required below 32F per AWS | AWS D1.1 | Base metal preheat to 70F minimum for structural steel |
| Soil compaction | Do not compact frozen soil | Geotech specs | Frozen lumps prevent proper compaction |
| Excavation | Frost depth affects digging | Local codes | Foundations must extend below frost line |
| Caulking/sealants | 40F (4C) minimum typically | Manufacturer TDS | Adhesion and cure both temperature-dependent |
| Epoxy coatings | 50F (10C) and rising | Manufacturer | Chemical reaction slows/stops below threshold |

### Worker Cold Stress (OSHA Guidance)

| Wind Chill | Risk Level | Required Action |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| > 25F | Normal | Standard PPE |
| 10-25F | Caution | Warm break area, buddy system |
| -10 to 10F | Warning | Limit exposure to 30 min, mandatory warm-up breaks |
| < -10F | Danger | Limit exposure to 15 min, consider work stoppage |
| < -35F | Extreme | Non-emergency outdoor work should be suspended |

## Temperature — Hot Weather

### Impact Thresholds

| Condition | Threshold | Impact Level | Primary Concern |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Warm | 80-90F (27-32C) | LOW | Concrete set time shortens, worker hydration |
| Hot | 90-100F (32-38C) | MODERATE | Concrete protection required, heat illness risk |
| Very hot | 100-110F (38-43C) | HIGH | Serious heat illness risk, concrete challenging |
| Extreme heat | > 110F (> 43C) | CRITICAL | Consider suspending exterior work |

### Activity-Specific Hot Weather Restrictions

| Activity | Temperature Concern | Standard | Required Action |
|----------|-------------------|----------|----------------|
| Concrete placement | Above 90F (32C) | ACI 305R | Hot weather plan: ice in mix, retarder, wet cure immediately |
| Concrete - max temp | Concrete temp not to exceed 90F (32C) at placement | ACI 301 | Cool aggregates, use ice, shade mixer, schedule night pours |
| Concrete curing | Rapid moisture loss above 90F | ACI 308 | Apply curing compound within 10 min of finishing; fog spray |
| Asphalt paving | Surface temp > 140F (60C) - worker burn risk | Industry practice | Morning paving, shade when possible |
| Roofing | Surface temp > 150F (65C) on dark roofs | Industry practice | Early start, stop by 2PM, hydration stations |
| Steel erection | Steel surface burns above 120F (49C) | Industry practice | Gloves required, tool handles hot |
| Painting | Most paints max 100F (38C) application | Manufacturer TDS | Flash drying causes defects; schedule early AM |
| Epoxy/urethane | Pot life shortens dramatically above 90F | Manufacturer | Small batches, shade, schedule for cooler hours |
| Waterproofing | Excessive heat causes premature cure | Manufacturer | Apply in shade or cooler hours |

### Worker Heat Illness Prevention (OSHA/Cal-OSHA)

| Heat Index | Risk Level | Required Actions |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| < 91F | Lower | Water, rest, shade available |
| 91-103F | Moderate | Encourage drinking water every 15 min, rest breaks in shade |
| 103-115F | High | Mandatory water (1 qt/hr), 15-min rest per hour in shade, buddy system |
| > 115F | Very High/Extreme | Mandatory 15-min rest per 45 min work, close medical observation, consider suspension |

**Cal-OSHA Title 8, Section 3395** (most stringent state standard):
- Water: 1 quart per worker per hour, accessible at all times
- Shade: Available when temp > 80F, enough for all workers on break
- Rest: 10-min cool-down rest per 2 hours when > 95F
- High-heat procedures (> 95F): buddy system, pre-shift meetings, emergency response plan

## Wind

### Impact Thresholds

| Condition | Sustained Speed | Gust Speed | Impact Level |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| Light | < 15 mph | < 25 mph | LOW — Normal operations |
| Moderate | 15-25 mph | 25-35 mph | MODERATE — Some restrictions |
| Strong | 25-35 mph | 35-50 mph | HIGH — Significant restrictions |
| Severe | 35-50 mph | 50-65 mph | CRITICAL — Limited operations |
| Extreme | > 50 mph | > 65 mph | STOP — Suspend all outdoor work |

### Activity-Specific Wind Restrictions

| Activity | Max Wind Speed | Regulatory/Industry Basis | Notes |
|----------|---------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Crane operations - mobile | Manufacturer rated, typically 20-30 mph | OSHA 1926.1417, ASME B30.5 | Varies by crane, load, boom length; consult load chart |
| Crane operations - tower | Per manufacturer, typically 45 mph (operational) | OSHA 1926.1417 | Free-slew (weathervane) at higher speeds |
| Steel erection | Suspend when sustained > 30 mph | OSHA 1926.752(c) | Tag lines required; no hoisting in gusts > 35 mph |
| Scaffold use | Do not use when sustained > 25 mph | OSHA 1926.451(f)(12) | Exception: wind screens installed and scaffold redesigned for load |
| Roofing - membrane | 15-25 mph depending on system | NRCA / Manufacturer | Loose-laid membranes can be windborne; secure daily |
| Roofing - hot asphalt | 15 mph maximum | Industry practice | Wind-blown hot asphalt = severe burn hazard |
| Concrete placement | 25 mph sustained causes rapid evaporation | ACI 305R | Use windbreaks, evaporation retarders, fog spray |
| Exterior painting | 15 mph typically | MPI standards | Wind causes overspray, uneven coverage, debris in wet paint |
| Spray fireproofing | 15 mph in open areas | Industry practice | Overspray, uneven coverage, clean-up issues |
| Aerial lifts (boom/scissor) | Per manufacturer, typically 28-30 mph | OSHA 1926.453, ANSI A92.5 | Operator must lower platform and stop in high winds |
| Personnel hoists | 35 mph typically | OSHA 1926.552 | Manufacturer specifications govern |
| Dust control | > 15 mph triggers dust concerns | EPA, SWPPP | Water suppression, windscreens required |
| Glass/curtain wall install | 15-20 mph for handling | Industry practice | Large panels become sails; one gust can be fatal |
| Panel erection (precast, tilt-up) | 20 mph typically | Industry practice, crane limits | Large surface area panels extremely wind-sensitive |
| Formwork stripping | > 30 mph can topple stripped forms | Industry practice | Brace forms before stripping, sequence carefully |
| Excavation/trenching | > 25 mph | Industry practice | Soil cave-in risk increases; trench boxes move |
| Welding | 5 mph at weld point without shielding | AWS D1.1 | Use wind screens; shield gas welding at lower speeds |

## Snow and Ice

### Impact Thresholds

| Condition | Amount | Impact Level | Primary Concern |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Light snow | < 1 in | LOW | Slippery surfaces, visibility |
| Moderate snow | 1-4 in | MODERATE | Access impaired, structural loading |
| Heavy snow | 4-8 in | HIGH | Site shutdown likely, structural loading |
| Blizzard | > 8 in + high wind | CRITICAL | Full shutdown, monitor structural loads |
| Ice storm | Any accumulation | HIGH-CRITICAL | Extremely hazardous, power outages |

### Construction Impacts

| Impact Area | Action Required |
|-------------|----------------|
| Site access | Plow/salt roads and parking; maintain emergency access at all times |
| Walking surfaces | Sand/salt walkways, stairs; remove ice from scaffolds, ladders, platforms |
| Structural loading | Monitor snow loads on temporary structures, formwork, incomplete framing |
| Concrete | Do not place on frozen subgrade; protect fresh concrete from freezing |
| Equipment | Warm up engines; check hydraulic fluid viscosity; inspect wire rope for ice |
| Excavations | Inspect for ice lens formation, frozen sidewall collapse |
| Temporary enclosures | Check for snow/ice load accumulation on temporary roofs/tarps |
| Cranes | Remove ice from boom, check for frozen sheaves, wire rope |
| Materials | Protect stored materials from snow/ice damage; check deliveries |

## Lightning

### OSHA and Industry Standard

| Condition | Action | Basis |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Thunder heard or lightning seen within 30 miles | Alert crews, prepare to stop | 30-30 Rule |
| Lightning within 10 miles (flash-to-bang < 50 sec) | Suspend crane operations, steel erection | OSHA guidance, ASME B30.5 |
| Lightning within 6 miles (flash-to-bang < 30 sec) | Suspend ALL outdoor work | 30-30 Rule (NOAA) |
| 30 minutes after last observed lightning | Resume work | 30-30 Rule |

### High-Risk Activities During Lightning

- Crane operations (highest risk — tall metal structure)
- Steel erection (workers on metal at height)
- Scaffold work (elevated metal structure)
- Roofing (elevated, exposed)
- Pile driving (tall equipment, metal)
- Any work on tall structures or metal framing

## Humidity

### Impact on Construction Activities

| Activity | Max Relative Humidity | Impact |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Painting | 85% RH (surface must be > 5F above dew point) | Adhesion failure, blistering, slow dry |
| Epoxy application | 85% RH (surface must be > 5F above dew point) | Amine blush, incomplete cure |
| Concrete finishing | High RH slows evaporation — beneficial in hot weather | In cold weather, slows set and strength gain |
| Welding | High humidity increases hydrogen cracking risk | Use low-hydrogen electrodes, preheat |
| Flooring | Per manufacturer, typically 65-75% RH max for substrate | Moisture vapor emission > 3 lbs/1000 SF/24 hr = problem |
| Wood flooring | 30-50% RH ideal, wood must acclimate | Expansion/contraction with humidity changes |
| Spray fireproofing | Excessive humidity delays drying | May need dehumidification |
| Drywall taping | High humidity extends dry time | Schedule accordingly; ventilate |

| > 1/4 mile | Normal | Standard operations |

## Composite Weather Scoring for Daily Log

Use this scoring system to rate the day's weather impact on a 1-5 scale:

| Score | Label | Description | Typical Production Impact |
|-------|-------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Ideal | Clear, 50-80F, calm winds, low humidity | 100% production |
| 2 | Good | Minor weather — light breeze, warm but manageable | 90-100% production |
| 3 | Fair | Some impact — moderate heat/cold, intermittent rain, gusty | 70-90% production |
| 4 | Poor | Significant impact — heavy rain, extreme heat/cold, high wind | 30-70% production |
| 5 | Lost Day | Weather prevents productive work | 0-30% production |

### Weather Day Documentation (for delay claims)

For each weather day (Score 4-5), document:
1. **Conditions** — temperature, precipitation, wind speed (from weather station data)
2. **Start/stop times** — when work was stopped and resumed
3. **Affected activities** — which activities on critical path were impacted
4. **Crew impact** — which crews were sent home or reassigned
5. **Mitigation** — what was done to mitigate (moved to interior work, covered work, etc.)
6. **Weather source** — nearest weather station, on-site gauge readings

### Contract Weather Day Provisions

Most contracts define allowable weather days. Common frameworks:

- **Fixed allowance**: Contract includes X weather days per month (historical average)
- **Comparison to normal**: Weather days exceeding 10-year average for the location are excusable
- **Activity-specific**: Only weather days affecting critical path activities are excusable
- **Documentation requirement**: Must be logged within 24 hours to preserve claim rights

Always check the specific contract provisions (typically in Supplementary Conditions or Division 01) for the project's weather day policy.
