# General Conditions Checklist

> Comprehensive checklist for estimating Division 01 / General Conditions costs.
> Costs expressed as percentage of hard costs and/or monthly rates where applicable.
> Typical GC range: 8-15% of hard construction costs (varies by project size, type, complexity).

---

## 1. Project Staff

| Item | Description | Typical Cost | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Project Executive | Part-time oversight, executive decisions | $2,000-6,000/mo | 10-25% allocation; large projects only |
| Project Manager | Full-time contract admin, schedule, cost control | $10,000-18,000/mo | Full-time on projects > $5M |
| Assistant PM | Supports PM on large projects | $7,000-13,000/mo | Projects > $15M typically |
| Superintendent | Full-time field supervision | $10,000-20,000/mo | Required on every project; senior super for complex |
| Assistant Superintendent | Supports super on large/multi-phase projects | $7,000-14,000/mo | Projects > $10M or multiple buildings |
| MEP Coordinator | Coordinates mechanical, electrical, plumbing | $8,000-15,000/mo | Projects > $10M with significant MEP |
| Project Engineer | Submittals, RFIs, quantity tracking | $6,000-11,000/mo | Junior position; often combined with APM |
| Safety Manager | Full-time site safety | $7,000-14,000/mo | Required by OSHA on larger sites; part-time on smaller |
| QC Manager | Quality control oversight | $7,000-13,000/mo | Government/institutional projects; DOD/USACE require |
| Field Office Admin | Administrative support | $4,000-7,000/mo | Large projects; scheduling, filing, documentation |
| BIM Manager/Coordinator | BIM execution, clash detection | $8,000-15,000/mo | Projects requiring BIM; may be part-time |

**Staff burden rate:** Add 30-45% to base salary for benefits, taxes, workers comp, vehicle.

**Typical total staff cost:** 3-6% of hard costs.

### Project Size Effect on GC %

| Project Value | Typical GC % | Why |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| < $1M | 15-22% | Fixed overhead spread over small base |
| $1M - $5M | 12-18% | One superintendent, part-time PM |
| $5M - $15M | 10-15% | Full-time super + PM |
| $15M - $50M | 8-12% | Economies of scale, full staff |
| $50M - $100M | 7-10% | Dedicated large project team |
| > $100M | 6-9% | Multiple supers, dedicated team, but massive base |

## Estimating Tips

1. **Build the GC estimate bottom-up** — list every line item with duration and monthly cost rather than using a flat percentage. The percentage is a sanity check, not the estimate.

2. **Duration drives cost** — a 12-month project with 1 superintendent costs roughly the same for staff whether the project is $5M or $10M. Longer projects have proportionally higher GC.

3. **Count the months** — multiply monthly items (staff, trailers, equipment rental) by actual project duration including mobilization and closeout.

4. **Don't forget burden** — staff costs should include benefits, taxes, vehicle, phone, PPE. Typically 30-45% on top of base salary.

5. **Bonds scale with contract value** — bond rates decrease on larger projects (sliding scale). Get actual bond rate from surety.

6. **Insurance rates vary** — get actual rates from your insurance broker. EMR (Experience Modification Rate) significantly affects cost.

7. **Temporary facilities accumulate** — small monthly costs add up. A $1,500/month trailer for 18 months = $27,000 plus setup and removal.

8. **Closeout takes time** — budget 1-2 months of reduced staff (superintendent + PM) for punch list and closeout after substantial completion.

9. **Weather days cost money** — lost productivity days still carry GC overhead. Budget for realistic weather delays by location.

10. **Contingency** — most GC budgets include 3-5% contingency within general conditions for unexpected conditions, scope gaps, and minor changes.
