# Overhead & Profit Markup Rates Reference

> Industry-standard markup rates for change orders by project type, contract type, and tier.
> These are negotiation ranges — actual rates are governed by the specific contract.

---

## Standard OH&P Markup Structure

A typical change order markup is applied in layers:

```
Direct Cost (Labor + Material + Equipment)
  + Subcontractor Markup (if applicable)
  + Contractor's Overhead (job-site + home office)
  + Contractor's Profit
  + Bond Premium (on increased contract value)
  = Total Change Order Amount
```

---

## Markup Rates by Contract Type

### Lump Sum / Stipulated Sum (AIA A101)

Most common contract type. All-in pricing, highest markups because contractor carries the most risk.

| Cost Tier | Overhead | Profit | Total OH&P | Notes |
|-----------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| GC self-performed work | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Overhead includes job-site and home office |
| GC markup on subcontractor work | 5-10% | 3-5% | 8-15% | GC coordinates, manages, and is responsible for sub's work |
| Subcontractor self-performed | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Sub's own overhead and profit |
| Sub-sub (2nd tier) | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Sub-subcontractor's own markup |

**Typical compounded markup example (sub-performed change):**
- Sub's direct cost: $10,000
- Sub OH&P at 20%: $2,000 → Sub total: $12,000
- GC markup on sub at 10%: $1,200
- **Total to Owner: $13,200** (32% over direct cost)

---

### Cost Plus / Time & Materials (AIA A102)

Contractor is reimbursed for actual costs plus an agreed fee. Lower markups because contractor risk is lower.

| Cost Tier | Overhead | Profit / Fee | Total Markup | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| GC self-performed work | 8-12% | 3-8% | 11-20% | Often a fixed fee or % of cost |
| GC markup on subcontractor work | 3-7% | 2-5% | 5-12% | Coordination and management only |
| Subcontractor work | 8-12% | 3-8% | 11-20% | Per sub's contract terms |

---

### Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) (AIA A133)

Hybrid of lump sum and cost-plus. Contractor has capped price with shared savings. Markups typically negotiated.

| Cost Tier | Overhead | Profit | Total OH&P | Notes |
|-----------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| GC self-performed work | 8-12% | 5-8% | 13-20% | Within GMP; changes to GMP have higher rates |
| GC markup on sub work | 3-8% | 2-5% | 5-13% | Sub costs are open-book |
| Changes to GMP (owner-directed) | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Rate specified in contract |
| Subcontractor work | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Per sub's agreement |

**GMP savings split** (typical): 50/50 or 75/25 (Owner/Contractor). Defined in contract.

---

### Construction Manager at Risk (CMR)

Similar to GMP but with pre-construction services phase. Markups typically lower due to collaborative relationship.

| Cost Tier | Overhead | Profit | Total OH&P | Notes |
|-----------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| GC/CM self-performed | 8-12% | 4-8% | 12-20% | CM fee often covers profit |
| CM markup on sub work | 3-7% | 2-4% | 5-11% | CM fee may be separate flat amount |
| Pre-construction services | Hourly or fixed fee | — | N/A | Separate from construction phase |

**CM Fee structure alternatives:**
- Fixed fee: Negotiated lump sum (not affected by changes)
- % of cost of work: 2-5% typically
- Hybrid: Fixed base + % of savings

---

### Design-Build

Single point of responsibility for design and construction. Markups may be higher because scope risk is on the DB entity.

| Cost Tier | Overhead | Profit | Total OH&P | Notes |
|-----------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| DB entity self-performed | 12-18% | 5-10% | 17-28% | Includes design coordination risk |
| DB markup on sub/consultant | 5-12% | 3-7% | 8-19% | Design changes may require redesign |
| Owner-directed changes | 10-15% | 5-10% | 15-25% | Owner changes to DB contract |

---

### Unit Price Contracts

Pre-agreed unit prices with quantity adjustments. Limited markup flexibility.

| Scenario | Markup Approach | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Quantity within bid range (typically +/-25%) | Unit price governs | No additional markup |
| Quantity exceeds bid range | Renegotiated unit price | Typically 10-20% OH&P on difference |
| New line items (not in bid) | Standard OH&P per contract | 15-25% typical |
| Quantity reduction > 25% | Contractor may claim lost overhead | Impact on fixed costs |

## Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) Rates

For federal government contracts (USACE, NAVFAC, GSA, VA):

### FAR 15.404-4 — Profit/Fee Ranges

| Factor | Range | Consideration |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Contractor effort (labor-intensive) | 3-9% profit | Complexity of work, skill required |
| Contractor effort (material-intensive) | 1-4% profit | Lower risk on material-heavy work |
| Contract cost risk | 0-7% | Degree of cost risk borne by contractor |
| Investment in facilities/equipment | -2% to +2% | Contractor investment relevant to contract |
| Performance | -2% to +2% | Past performance record |
| Federal socioeconomic programs | 0-0.5% | Small business, HUBZone, etc. |

**Typical total profit on federal change orders:** 6-10% on self-performed, 3-5% on subcontracted.

### FAR-Referenced Overhead Rates

| Category | Typical Rate | Basis |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Job-site overhead | 8-12% | Direct field supervision, temp facilities |
| Home office overhead (G&A) | 3-8% | Corporate overhead allocated to project |
| Combined overhead | 10-18% | Total overhead on direct costs |
| Profit | 5-10% | Applied after overhead |
| Bond | 0.5-2% | Applied on total including OH&P |

## Bond Premium on Changes

Performance and payment bond premiums increase with the contract value:

| Contract Value Tier | Bond Rate (on incremental value) |
|--------------------|--------------------------------|
| First $100K | 2.5-3.5% |
| $100K - $500K | 1.5-2.5% |
| $500K - $2.5M | 1.0-2.0% |
| $2.5M - $5M | 0.75-1.5% |
| $5M - $10M | 0.5-1.0% |
| Over $10M | 0.5-0.75% |

**Rule of thumb:** Budget 0.5-1.5% of the change order value for bond premium.

The bond company must be notified of changes that increase the contract value. Most bonds include an automatic increase provision up to a percentage (typically 10-25%) of the original bond amount.

## Tiered Markup Structures

Some contracts specify different markup rates based on change order value to prevent excessive markups on large changes:

### Example Tiered Structure

| Change Value | OH&P Rate |
|-------------|-----------|
| First $25,000 | 20% |
| $25,001 - $100,000 | 15% |
| $100,001 - $500,000 | 12% |
| Over $500,000 | 10% |

### Example Cap Structure

| Component | Maximum Allowed |
|-----------|----------------|
| GC overhead | 10% of direct cost |
| GC profit | 5% of direct cost + overhead |
| Sub overhead | 10% of sub's direct cost |
| Sub profit | 5% of sub's direct cost + overhead |
| GC markup on sub work | 5% of sub's total |
| Bond | Actual cost |

## Credit Change Orders (Deductive Changes)

When work is deleted from the contract:

| Approach | Description | Typical Application |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Full credit including OH&P | Owner receives credit for direct cost + markup | Most favorable to owner; some contracts require this |
| Direct cost credit only | Owner receives credit for direct cost; contractor retains OH&P | Contractor argument: overhead was already incurred |
| Reduced OH&P on credits | Credit at lower markup than additive changes | Compromise position; e.g., 10% OH&P on credits vs 20% on additions |
| Net change evaluation | Combine additions and deletions, apply markup to net | Prevents double-markup situations |

**AIA A201 (2017), Section 7.3.3.3:** "In calculating adjustments to the Contract Sum, the terms 'cost' and 'costs' ... shall not include the Contractor's overhead and profit."

**Key principle:** The contract language governs. Review the specific change order clause before applying any markup.

## Negotiation Guidelines

### For Owners/CMs

1. **Require detailed cost backup** — labor hours x rates, material quantities x unit costs, equipment hours x rates
2. **Audit labor rates** — verify against certified payroll or Davis-Bacon rates (public work)
3. **Check material costs** — request supplier quotes, not catalog pricing
4. **Verify equipment rates** — use Blue Book or AGC rates, not rental house retail
5. **Watch for double-dipping** — overhead items already in general conditions should not be re-charged
6. **Negotiate markups upfront** — define rates in the contract before issues arise
7. **Set time limits** — require COR submission within 14-21 days of direction
8. **Require CPM fragments** — for any change claiming schedule impact

### For Contractors

1. **Document everything** — photos, daily logs, labor records before, during, and after the change
2. **Separate direct costs clearly** — labor, material, equipment, sub each itemized
3. **Include impact costs** — disruption, acceleration, inefficiency, ripple effects on adjacent work
4. **Submit timely** — late CORs weaken the claim and allow owners to argue waiver
5. **Reference the contract** — cite the specific markup rates and change order provisions
6. **Include escalation** — if the change will be performed in the future, include material/labor escalation
7. **Protect time** — always request schedule extension with cost changes; time costs money
8. **Cumulative impact** — multiple changes create compound inefficiency; document the cumulative effect

## Notes

- **Always check the contract first.** These are industry ranges; the contract controls.
- **Markups compound.** A 20% sub markup + 10% GC markup = 32% over direct cost, not 30%.
- **Taxes are separate.** Sales tax on materials is a direct cost, not part of OH&P.
- **Small changes cost more.** Mobilization, coordination, and disruption make small changes disproportionately expensive per unit.
- **Disputed markups delay payment.** Negotiate rates upfront to avoid disputes later.
- **Cumulative change impact.** After 10-15% of contract value in changes, productivity loss claims become more supportable.
