## Category 1: Design Clarification

### Template 1.1 — Drawing Conflict Resolution

**Use when:** Two or more drawings show conflicting information (dimensions, details, notes).

```
RESPONSE TO RFI #[RFI_NUMBER]

Subject: Conflicting Information Between [DRAWING_A] and [DRAWING_B]

The Architect/Engineer has reviewed the referenced drawings and confirms the following:

1. CONFLICT IDENTIFIED: [DRAWING_A] shows [CONDITION_A] while [DRAWING_B] shows [CONDITION_B] at [LOCATION].

2. DESIGN INTENT: The intent is [DESCRIBE_CORRECT_CONDITION]. [DRAWING_A / DRAWING_B] governs.

3. CLARIFICATION: [Provide specific direction — dimensions, materials, details to follow].

4. AFFECTED DRAWINGS: The following drawings will be revised in the next bulletin/ASI:
   - [DRAWING_A]: [Description of revision]
   - [DRAWING_B]: [Description of revision]

5. COST/SCHEDULE IMPACT: [The Contractor shall proceed per this direction with no anticipated cost or schedule impact / The Contractor shall provide a cost and schedule impact proposal within [X] business days.]

6. SPECIFICATION REFERENCE: [CSI Section XX XX XX, paragraph X.X.X]

This response does not authorize any change in contract price or time. If the Contractor believes this direction results in a change, submit a COR per General Conditions [SECTION].
```

## Usage Notes

### General RFI Response Best Practices

1. **Respond within contractual timeframe** — typically 5-10 business days. Track and log response dates.
2. **Be specific** — reference exact drawing numbers, detail numbers, grid lines, specification paragraphs.
3. **One RFI = one topic** — reject bundled RFIs and ask for separate submissions.
4. **Flag cost impacts early** — never let the Contractor claim "constructive acceleration" because of late CO processing.
5. **Attach sketches when helpful** — label as "SK" (sketch) and note they do not modify contract documents unless formalized.
6. **Use consistent language** — "The Contractor shall..." not "You should..." or "Please..."
7. **Preserve the record** — every RFI response is a potential exhibit in a claim. Write accordingly.
8. **Don't redesign via RFI** — if the response requires significant redesign, issue an ASI or bulletin instead.

### When to Escalate Instead of Respond

- Potential design error with significant cost impact → route to design team for formal evaluation
- Differing site condition claim → route to Owner and legal/claims
- Life safety issue → immediate field directive, formal documentation to follow
- Scope dispute between trades → project management/CM resolution meeting
- Impacts critical path by more than 5 days → schedule review with all stakeholders

### RFI Tracking Fields

Every RFI response should track:
- RFI number, date received, date due, date responded
- Subject, location (building/floor/grid)
